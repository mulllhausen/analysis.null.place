A cookie is a small piece of text that allows a website to recognize your device and maintain a consistent, cohesive experience throughout multiple sessions. The cookies used in this blog are described below.

google analytics cookies

These cookies are saved by Google and provide feedback to me about the types of people who are visiting my blog. All visitor data is anonymous - Google does not disclose the identities of individual visitors. However the following data is available:

  • The number of visitors over a given time period, and for which pages
  • Which country visitors are browsing from
  • What device visitors are browsing with

If you would like more information on how Google Analytics uses cookies to measure user-interactions on websites then please click here.

adsense cookies

The ads in this blog are managed by Google AdSense. AdSense uses cookies as part of their ad serving process. Prior visits to this blog or other websites determine which ads you see. If you would like to opt out of personalized advertising then please click here to visit their Ads Settings page. Alternatively, you can opt out of personalised third-party advertisors' cookies by visiting www.aboutads.info.

facebook-comments cookies

These cookies are only loaded if you open the Facebook comments plugin. They enable you to leave comments on articles using your facebook account. If you would like more information on how Facebook uses cookies the please click here.

disqus-comments cookies

These cookies are only loaded if you open the Disqus comments plugin. They enable you to leave comments on articles using your Disqus account. If you would like more information on how Disqus uses cookies the please click here.

cookie policy agreement cookie

When you click the "ok" button in the footer, to accept using cookies on this blog, then a cookie is saved. This ensures you only see the message once.
